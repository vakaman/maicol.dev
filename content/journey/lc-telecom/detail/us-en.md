L&C Telecom was where everything started. I worked in technical support, the classic support role that often asked customers to reboot the router so we could restore connectivity and continue troubleshooting.

On a daily basis I helped customers whose internet service was failing, bringing their connection back to normal, but I also worked on the commercial side by presenting plans and closing sales with new customers. That mix of operations and business gave me an early practical view of technology.

That was also where I had my first real success case with software development. After learning how an HTML form worked and how to send data to a server with `POST` and `GET`, I built a system I called `agenda`.

The system could schedule installations, maintenance visits, equipment removals, and address changes. It was simple, but it had real impact: it made my work easier and improved the routine of the installers and technicians at the time.
