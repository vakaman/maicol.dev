# A new restart

After spending some time teaching and another stretch working as an internet installer assistant, I got my first formal job with a signed contract at L&C Telecom. It was a very small company, with around 10 employees, but at that moment, that was already enough for me.

# Much more than support

I worked in technical support, the kind of role that often asked customers to reboot the router to restore the connection and continue the diagnosis. As usually happens in small companies, my responsibilities did not stop there. I handled sales, blocked and reactivated customers, built the company website and, as I gained more experience working with networks, I started configuring the provider's routers, defining configuration standards for customers, managing the installers, and so on.

# The operations problem

As the process evolved, I realized how difficult it was to manage the installers. We did not have a system to control the jobs each installer needed to do in the following days, so I had to send emails with a lot of information, such as who the customer was, the address, what problem they had and, in installation cases, also the contracted plan and whether they had purchased wi-fi. We are talking about a time when the plans being sold were 128k, 256k, and 1mb, so... yes, I am getting old already hahaha.

# The urge to solve it

That was when a thought came to me: I already knew how to build websites, because in the course I took at Maxper I had learned the basics needed to create a static site with HTML and CSS. I knew nothing about JavaScript or PHP, but I knew those things existed. Even so, I was still very far from mastering the basics. At that time, I did not even know what an "if" meant.

I was taken over by a very strong urge to solve that problem: create a place where the installer could log in and see the tasks assigned to him during the week. The only thing that went through my head was: if someone has the ability to do it, then it is possible. Nobody could convince me that I could not do the same. After that, something in my mind simply clicked.

# Learning the hard way

I managed to get a notebook that weighed more than a sack of cement, which I probably traded for something I had, and I carried that beast to work every day. Since I did not have internet at home, I could not download anything there, so that notebook helped me a lot during that period. I downloaded YouTube videos teaching how to build a contact form in PHP, took them home, and copied everything the guy in the video was doing. After a million attempts, it would magically work, and I could not contain my desire to learn more. I was completely hooked.

# The system that brought order

Some time passed and, yes, I made it. The installers would log into the system I had built, enter their username and password, and see only the installations, maintenance visits, address changes, and removals assigned to them. I, with an administrator account, could manage everything, with logs and city-based separation. It was very ugly, but to me it was simply perfect. I had finally managed to bring order to the operation and make the schedules actually work.

# The value that remained

After a while, I ended up leaving the company, but my system kept running there properly for many years. Honestly, I do not know what happened to it after that, but I am sure it delivered a lot of value to the company.
