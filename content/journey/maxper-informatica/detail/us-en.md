# Young apprentice

The year was 2005, and I was 15 years old. I was young, full of doubts about life, and had the feeling that I did not have a clear direction yet. That was me, starting as a student at Maxper. I was fascinated by everything I was living through. School life was fun in its own way, but nothing compared to learning about the world of computers. I know that sounds strange today, but I thought the power of the mouse was something magical hehehe. I had already had some contact with computers at my aunt and uncle's house, but that was the first time I formally had a learning journey in this field.

# Small big discovery
During the training, I realized how much I liked technology. That was where I learned the basics: Windows, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, HTML, CSS, Photo-Paint, Corel Draw, and internet use, that classic course meant to "prepare" you to use a computer. Before long, I discovered that I had a great ability to learn, and as soon as I understood something and could reproduce it, I was already able to help my classmates. Little by little, I found myself becoming part of the training, and my instructor allowed me to help in other classes. I believe I can consider that my first informal job in the technology field.

# Not everything is easy
The school was about an hour away from my house and, for a long time, I had to walk there, but commitment is commitment. After some time, I managed to get a bike, and that made the commute much easier. I had to balance school and work because, whether I wanted it or not, life forced me to learn responsibility very early.

# Am I an instructor?
Since I did not have money to pay for new courses, I made my instructor an indecent proposal: I would help with the training sessions, and he would teach me for free and let me have access to the computers. And that is exactly how it went. I spent about a year working, teaching computer classes in exchange for internet access, contact with the computer, and the opportunity to learn for free.

At that time, my family did not have the financial means, and having a computer at home or even internet access was simply impossible. In a way, today I feel proud of what I lived through in that moment. I do not regret anything, and I am very happy for the opportunity I was given back then. I would do it all again.
