I started as a student at Maxper and, during the training, realized how much I liked technology. That was where I learned the basics: Windows, Word, Excel, PowerPoint, HTML, CSS, Photo-Paint, Corel Draw, and internet use, that classic course meant to "prepare" you to use a computer.

During the course, I realized I had a huge ease for learning, and as soon as I understood something and could reproduce it, I was already able to help my classmates. Before long, I found myself becoming part of the training, and my instructor allowed me to help in other classes. I believe I can consider that my first informal job in technology. The school was about an hour away from my house and, for a long time, I had to walk there, but commitment is commitment. After some time, I managed to get a bike, and that made the commute much easier.

Since I did not have money to pay for new courses, I made my instructor an indecent proposal: I would help with the training sessions, and he would teach me for free. And that is exactly how it went. I spent about a year working, teaching computer classes in exchange for having more contact with the computer and learning for free.

At that time, my family did not have the financial means, and having a computer at home or even internet access was simply impossible. In a way, today I feel proud of what I lived through in that moment. I would do it all again.
